Dieter Kindlmann

Dieter Kindlmann

Current
Madison Keys
Birthplace
Bremen, German
Level
Gold
Ger
  • Bio

Kindlmann transitioned into the world of tennis coaching after a 10 year playing career on the ATP tour. He won four titles at the Challenger level, reaching a career-high rank of No.130 in the process.

Upon retirement, Kindlmann began working as the hitting partner and assistant coach for former world No.1 Maria Sharapova. From there he started working as the primary coach for Anastasia Pavlyuchenkova, guiding her to a career-best quarterfinal appearance at Wimbledon in 2016. After helping her complete her fifth straight Top 30 season, Kindlmann moved on to briefly coach Great Britain’s Laura Robson, before landing his current job, with American No.3, Madison Keys.

Keys needed only two practices with Kindlmann before bringing him onto her team, full time. He serves as the day-to-day coach for Keys, overseeing her attempt to return to the Top 10 after reaching as high as No.7 in 2016. Thus far, the Kindlmann and Keys duo have reached two finals, winning in Stanford before finishing as the runner-up at the 2017 US Open, Keys’ best result at a Grand Slam to date.

Kindlemann has also coached three ATP athletes: Kevin Krawietz, Johannes Härteis and Hannes Wagner. When he is not traveling with Keys, he enjoys spending time with his family, listening to music and watching soccer.
